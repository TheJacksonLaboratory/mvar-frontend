MOUSE MUTANT RESOURCE DATABASE

For more than 80 years, the Mouse Mutant Resource at The Jackson Laboratory (JAX) has characterized naturally occurring variants causing Mendelian phenotypes in the vast population of laboratory mice that are maintained in JAX vivaria. Roughly 150 novel phenotypes arise in these colonies each year as expected based on known frequencies of spontaneous mutation and expected rates of fixation under selective breeding paradigms. The goal of forward genetics is to rapidly identify the genetic variants that cause heritable phenotypes. High throughput sequencing technologies and population scale variant data allow for rapid prioritization of the most plausible pathogenic mutations based on their frequency among these highly related, primarily isogenic strains of Mus musculus domesticus. The Mouse Mutant Database provides access to aggregated, annotated variant calls generated from high throughput sequencing of these strains with an emphasis on strains harboring recently acquired mutations that cause Mendelian disease phenotypes. Our V.2 dataset currently encompasses over 33.5 million SNP and INDEL variants and 11.6 million structural variants from ~450 samples representing more than 350 unique mouse strains. Database expansion and improvements for V.3 will include enhanced tools for structural variant visualization, as well as tools for direct upload and annotation of user-generated VCF files.
